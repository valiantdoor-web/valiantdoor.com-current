Dublin Garage Door Repair: Rebuilding a Cracked Center Stile

A Dublin homeowner had a double door that flexed and rattled near the middle every time it opened. Instead of selling a full door replacement, we reinforced the damaged section and rebuilt the center-stile support, so the door tracks straight again at a fraction of the cost.

What the homeowner noticed

The center of the door looked bowed, and the panel had started to crack around the strut area. Every time the opener ran, the middle of the door would shudder and make a loud rattle, and it was getting worse week to week.

What we found

The center stile, the vertical support that runs down the middle of the door, had failed at its fastener points, and the section had lost its rigidity. The bracket, strut, and hinge hardware in that area were working loose because the panel could no longer hold them steady.

The repair we recommended

We reinforced the damaged section with a section saver, installed a new bracket, strut, and hinge hardware, and re-riveted the center-stile support. Replacing the whole door was not necessary: reinforcing the section restored full strength while keeping the existing door. After the repair we cycled the door and confirmed it moved straight and quiet.
