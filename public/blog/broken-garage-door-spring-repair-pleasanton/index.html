How To Tell If The Spring Broke

Common Warning Signs

The biggest clues are a loud bang from the garage, a visible gap in the torsion spring, a door that suddenly feels too heavy, or an opener that hums but will not lift the door correctly.

What Not To Do

Do not force the door by hand and do not keep pressing the opener. A broken spring leaves the door unbalanced and can overload the opener or pull the cable system out of position.

Why Springs Fail

Most springs break from normal cycle wear. Heat, corrosion, poor balance, and incorrect spring sizing can shorten spring life and lead to earlier failure.

What Happens During Repair

Safe spring replacement includes matching the correct spring size, setting the right tension, checking cable alignment, balancing the door, and confirming the opener is not overworking the system afterward.

Same-Day Pleasanton Calls

Many broken spring calls in Pleasanton can be handled the same day when the schedule and correct spring size are available, especially for doors that are stuck closed or no longer safe to move.

Why The Whole Door System Gets Checked

Balance And Travel

After spring replacement, the door still has to lift evenly and travel smoothly so the opener is not forced to carry the entire load.

Cables And Drums

When a spring breaks, the cable path can shift. That is why drums, cables, brackets, and shaft alignment should be checked before the door goes back into normal use.

Safety Before Convenience

Even if the door can still move a little, the safer choice is to stop using it until the spring system is repaired and tested.

Frequently Asked Questions

What Are The First Signs Of A Broken Garage Door Spring?

Common signs include a loud bang from the garage, a door that suddenly feels heavy, a visible gap in the torsion spring, or an opener that strains without lifting the door.

Should I Keep Using The Opener If The Spring Broke?

No. If a spring is broken, stop using the opener because the door may be unbalanced and can damage the opener or become unsafe to move.

Can Broken Spring Repair Usually Happen The Same Day In Pleasanton?

In many cases yes, provided the schedule and the correct spring size are available. After replacement, the door should be rebalanced and safety tested before normal use.

Do Both Springs Need To Be Replaced Together?

Often yes, especially on a two-Spring system where both springs have similar age and cycle wear. Replacing both at the same time can help restore balanced performance.
